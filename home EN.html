NATIONAL INSTITUTE OF APOPA

"Towards Development and Progress through Education"

History

The National Institute of Apopa was founded on February 7, 1981, at Vicente Acosta School, starting with a single academic high school section and 44 students. Under the direction of Professor José Jerónimo Yánez and with a small team of teachers and administrative staff, the institution began to grow. In 1984, it acquired its own land, which allowed for the gradual expansion of facilities and educational offerings, including commercial and health high schools in 1988.

Over the years, the institute has stood out for its prestige and national recognition, providing comprehensive care to students in academic, artistic, sports, and cultural areas. Today, it has 68 teachers, 26 administrative staff, and serves 1,681 students, offering various technical and general high school programs. Since 2015, the institute has developed a renewed curriculum, offering Vocational Technical High School programs in Accounting Administration, Primary Health Care, Electronics, Software Development, and General High School.

High School Programs

The Vocational Technical High School in Accounting Administration aims to train professionals with solid skills and knowledge in the financial and accounting management of companies and institutions. This program prepares students in areas such as accounting, finance, administration, and auditing, enabling them to perform key tasks in the planning and control of an organization's economic resources.

Upon completing their studies, students will be able to carry out accounting records, perform financial analyses, manage accounting software, and participate in economic decision-making. In addition, they will have the opportunity to continue their higher education in related fields, such as public accounting, business administration, or economics.

The Electronics Technician graduates with a strong technical and humanistic education that allows them to enter the labor market, become a productive agent for the country, and promote their personal development.

They can perform electronic maintenance on all types of generic circuits that do not involve applications from specific sectors and can also venture into industry by supporting automated processes.

The Primary Health Care Technician is a mid-level technician prepared to participate in health promotion processes, Community Organization, Health Administration Activities, Epidemiological Surveillance, First Aid in Emergency situations, Disaster Prevention, and professional activities that allow them to function automatically.

This high school graduate can work in basic health care in hospital, outpatient, and home settings, supporting health services.

Students acquire general knowledge in all scientific subjects, such as Mathematics, Natural Sciences, Social Sciences, Language, and Literature, among others.

General High School students graduate in two years and are prepared to attend university in different fields. They also acquire basic skills to perform effectively in the labor and business sectors, especially if their goal is to pursue higher education or meet immediate job expectations in commerce, industry, or as micro-entrepreneurs, depending on their aspirations.
